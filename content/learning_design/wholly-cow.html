Project Overview

Wholly Cow is an innovative educational program designed to teach sustainable agriculture and food systems through an immersive, hands-on learning experience. The program connects learners with the complete farm-to-table process, focusing on ethical farming practices, environmental sustainability, and nutritional awareness.

Challenge

In an increasingly disconnected food system, many people lack understanding of where their food comes from and the environmental impact of food production. The challenge was to create an engaging learning experience that bridges this knowledge gap and inspires more conscious food choices.

Approach

The Wholly Cow program was developed using experiential learning principles, combining direct farm experiences with multimedia learning resources and practical cooking applications. The curriculum follows the journey of food production from soil preparation to meal creation.

Key Components

  • Immersive farm visits and hands-on agricultural activities
  • Interactive learning modules on sustainable farming practices
  • Food processing and preparation workshops
  • Environmental impact assessment tools
  • Nutritional education and meal planning
  • Community engagement and local food system exploration

Outcomes

The Wholly Cow program has been implemented in various educational settings, from K-12 schools to adult continuing education, with significant impacts:

  • Increased understanding of sustainable food systems (85% improvement in knowledge assessments)
  • Changed food purchasing behaviors among participants (62% reported making more sustainable choices)
  • Greater support for local agriculture (47% increase in local farm purchases)
  • Improved nutritional awareness and cooking skills

Program Expansion

Initially developed for in-person learning, the Wholly Cow program has expanded to include digital components that allow for broader participation. The program now includes virtual farm tours, interactive online learning modules, and remote cooking workshops that maintain the experiential nature of the original program while increasing accessibility.
